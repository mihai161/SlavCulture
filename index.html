A gopnik (Russian: гопник, tr. gopnik, ˈɡopnʲɪk; Ukrainian: гопники, romanized: hopnyky; Belarusian: гопнік, romanized: hopnik)[1] is a member of a subculture in Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, and other former Soviet republics ─ a young man (or a woman, sometimes known as a "gopnitsa") of lower-class background that usually lives in Russian suburban areas (usually Millennials)[2] and comes from a family of poor education and income.[3] The collective noun is gopota (Russian: гопота). The subculture of gopniks has its roots in the late Russian Empire and evolved during the 20th century in many cities in the Soviet Union.[4][5] As of the late 2010s, the subculture has faded for the most part, although youth gangs (such as the A.U.E.) that resemble gopniks still exist in Russia and in other Slavic and Baltic countries.
